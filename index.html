Hyderabad Famous Places

Charminar

Built in 1591 by Sultan Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah, the Charminar is the iconic heart of Hyderabad. This 56-meter-tall granite and limestone structure was originally constructed to commemorate the end of a devastating plague and to anchor the new city. It famously features four grand arches and four magnificent minarets.

Golconda

Located in western Hyderabad, Golconda Fort was built in 1143 by the Kakatiya dynasty as a mud fortress on a granite hill. It became the Qutb Shahi dynasty's capital in 1518, expanding into a magnificent granite citadel famous as a global diamond trading hub before falling to the Mughals in 1687.

Swarnagiri

The Swarnagiri Venkateswara Swamy Temple is a recently established spiritual and architectural marvel, located on Manepally Hills in Bhuvanagiri, about 47 kilometers from Hyderabad. Consecrated in March 2024, the temple was built by the Manepally family as an act of profound thanksgiving after a miraculous event.

Hussain Sagar

Hussain Sagar is a heart-shaped, artificial lake in Hyderabad built in 1562 AD during the Qutb Shahi dynasty by Ibrahim Quli Qutb Shah. It was named after the Sufi saint Hussain Shah Wali, who oversaw its engineering and connected the twin cities of Hyderabad and Secunderabad.

Ramoji Film City

Located on the outskirts of Hyderabad, Ramoji Film City is the world’s largest integrated film studio complex. Spanning roughly 2,000 acres, it was established in 1996 by media mogul Ramoji Rao and holds a Guinness World Record. It functions as a fully operational filmmaking facility and a massive tourist destination with extravagant sets, amusement parks, and adventure zones.
